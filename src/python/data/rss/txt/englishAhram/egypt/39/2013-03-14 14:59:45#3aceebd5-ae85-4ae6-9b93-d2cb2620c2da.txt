Giza governor sentenced to month in jail
Governor of Giza and leader of local city council sentenced to one month in jail and removed from jobs after being found guilty of financial mismanagement
Giza Governor Ali Abdel-Rahman and city council leader Ismail Riyad have been sentenced to one month in jail on financial mismanagement charges.
Giza misdemeanour court also ordered the officials be removed from their official posts and released on bail of LE500 pending an appeal.
The case was brought by a university professor who claimed he had not been paid money owed to him by the city council.
