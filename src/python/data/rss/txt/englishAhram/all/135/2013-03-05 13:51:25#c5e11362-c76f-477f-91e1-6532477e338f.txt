Egypt stocks plunge 2.2% in mid-Tuesday's trading
OCI's tax dispute with the Egyptian government exerts a negative impact on the stock market for the second consecutive day
Egypt’s stocks dip in Tuesday’s mid-trading, as investors’ fears persist due to the tax dispute between the government and Orascom Construction Industries.
The market benchmark EGX30 plunged significantly by 2.2 percent recording 5,256 points.
The broader index EGX70 also declined by 1.1 percent.
Around 85 shares, of 145 shares currently listed, seem to be heading down.
