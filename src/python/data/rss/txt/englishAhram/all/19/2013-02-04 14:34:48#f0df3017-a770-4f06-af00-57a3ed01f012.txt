Egypt economy grows 2.6 per cent in 1Q 2012/13: Finance minister
The Egyptian economy sees 2.6 growth in the first three months of the current fiscal year which ends in 30 June 2013
 
The Egyptian economy grew by 2.6 per cent in the first quarter of the fiscal year 2012/13 which began in July 2012, the ministry of finance said Monday, according to state news agency, MENA.
A year earlier, economy growth rate was 0.3 per cent.
The Egyptian economy grew 2.2 per cent in the preceding 2011/12 fiscal year. The World Bank has
predicted
the economy will expand by 2.6 per cent in 2012/13.
This rate is a significantly lower figure than estimated by the Egyptian government, which foresees a 3.5 to 4 per cent in the current fiscal year ending on 30 June 2013.
